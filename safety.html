Trail Safety & Hiking Tips

Your safety is our top priority. Use this checklist to prepare well and hike responsibly.

Safety Essentials

Check Weather

Review forecasts before you travel. Avoid exposed ridges and river crossings during storms or heavy rain.

Stay on Trails

Stick to marked routes to protect nature and reduce injury risk. If in doubt, ask your guide before deviating.

Hydrate & Fuel

Carry 1–2L water (more for hot days). Bring light snacks to keep energy steady.

Be Prepared

Bring a basic first-aid kit and personal medication. Tell someone your plan and expected return time.

What to Carry

  • Good shoes (trail/running shoes or boots)
  • Water (1–2L), light snacks
  • Hat, sunscreen, rain jacket (weather changes fast)
  • Warm layer for windy ridges
  • Charged phone + power bank
  • ID and some cash/mobile money
  • Small backpack
  • Any prescribed medication
