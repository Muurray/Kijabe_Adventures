What Is the Big Five?

Published 2026 • By Kijabe Adventures Team

The Big Five refers to five of Africa’s most iconic animals: the lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhino. The term originated from early hunters describing the most difficult animals to hunt on foot. Today, it represents the most sought-after wildlife sightings on safari.

The Big Five Animals

1. Lion

Known as the “King of the Jungle,” lions are social and powerful big cats.

2. African Elephant

The largest land animal on Earth, famous for intelligence and strong family bonds.

3. Cape Buffalo

Tough, unpredictable, and incredibly strong.

4. Leopard

Elusive and solitary, leopards are master climbers and hunters.

5. Rhinoceros

Both black and white rhinos can be found in Kenya, known for their massive horns and prehistoric appearance.

Where to See the Big Five in Kenya

  • Maasai Mara National Reserve
  • Amboseli National Park
  • Lake Nakuru National Park
  • Tsavo East/West

Spotting all Big Five animals is a major highlight of any Kenyan safari!
