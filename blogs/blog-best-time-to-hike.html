The Best Time to Hike Each Mountain in Kenya

Updated 2025 • Kijabe Adventures

Kenya offers some of the most diverse and scenic hiking experiences in Africa—from volcanic craters to rugged peaks and lush highland forests. But choosing the right season can make the difference between an enjoyable hike and a difficult one. In this guide, we break down the best time to hike each major mountain in Kenya so you can plan your adventure with confidence.

General Hiking Seasons in Kenya

In most regions, Kenya has two dry seasons and two rainy seasons:

  • Best Dry Seasons (Ideal for Hiking): January–February & June–September
  • Long Rains: March–May (Slippery, muddy trails)
  • Short Rains: October–December (Unpredictable weather)

1. Mount Kenya — Best Time: January–February & August–September

Mount Kenya, the country’s highest mountain and a UNESCO World Heritage site, offers world-class trekking and technical climbing.

Best Season

January–February and August–September provide clear skies, minimal rainfall, and great visibility.

Avoid

March–June and October–December due to heavy rains and slippery alpine terrain.

2. Mount Longonot — Best Time: June–September & January–February

This volcanic crater in Naivasha is perfect for day hikers looking for a moderate challenge.

Best months: June–September (cool and dry) and January–February (sunny but manageable).

Avoid: April–May when trails become muddy and steep sections are slippery.

3. Mount Kipipiri — Best Time: January–March

Mount Kipipiri is located within the Aberdare ecosystem and is known for its rolling ridges and quiet forest trails.

Best season: January–March offers clear paths and comfortable temperatures.

Avoid: Long rains (March–June) due to overgrown vegetation and muddy tracks.

4. Aberdare Ranges — Best Time: January–February & July–October

Known for lush forests, cold misty air, and wildlife sightings, the Aberdares offer multiple hiking peaks including Satima and Elephant Hill.

Best Season: January–February for warm and dry days. July–October is also favorable but slightly cooler.

Avoid: April–June (very muddy, thick fog).

5. Ngong Hills — Best Time: All Year (Best: June–September)

Ngong Hills is the perfect beginner-to-intermediate ridge hike close to Nairobi.

Best Season: June–September (cool and windy). Good year-round: Clear skies almost every month.

6. Mount Ol Doinyo Eburru — Best Time: July–October

Eburru Forest offers volcanic peaks, hot steam vents, and thick forest trails.

Best Season: July–October (drier trails, good views).

7. Menengai Crater — Best Time: January–March & July–October

This massive caldera in Nakuru is suitable for all hiking levels.

Best Season: January–March & July–October for dry, scenic hikes.
