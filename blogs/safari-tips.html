10 Essential Safari Hiking Tips for Beginners

Published on February 15, 2026 • By Kijabe Adventures Team

Safari hiking in Kenya is an incredible experience, but preparation is key. Whether you're heading to Hell's Gate or Amboseli, these 10 tips will help you make the most of your adventure.

1. Start Early in the Morning

Most wildlife is active during early morning hours. Starting early improves visibility, comfort, and safety.

2. Invest in Proper Footwear

Good hiking boots prevent injuries and support your feet on uneven terrain.

3. Bring Plenty of Water

Always carry enough water—3 to 4 liters per day during a safari hike.

4. Use Sun Protection

Kenya’s sun is intense. Use sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing.

5. Follow Your Guide’s Instructions

Your guide understands wildlife behavior and terrain. Always follow their lead.

6. Pack Light but Smart

Carry only essentials: water, snacks, basic first aid, and a light jacket.

7. Wear Neutral Colors

Earth tones help you blend into nature and avoid startling animals.

8. Know Your Limits

Choose hiking trails that match your fitness level and avoid overexertion.

9. Respect Wildlife Distance

Never approach animals. Use binoculars for safe viewing.

10. Practice Leave-No-Trace

Take all your trash with you and preserve Kenya’s natural beauty.
