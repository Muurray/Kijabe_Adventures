10 Best Day Hikes Near Nairobi (2026 Guide)

By Kijabe Adventures Team Updated July 2026 7–10 min read

Home > Blog > Best Day Hikes Near Nairobi

Nairobi offers some of Kenya’s best day hikes—perfect for weekend explorers, fitness lovers, and nature enthusiasts. Whether you want waterfalls, panoramic hills, dense forests, or wildlife-filled trails, you don’t have to travel far.

Why Nairobi Is Perfect for Day Hikes

Nairobi’s strategic location places several nature reserves, mountains, and forests within 30–90 minutes of the city. Most trails are beginner-friendly, affordable, and accessible by public or private transport.

1. Kijabe Hills

Located about one hour from Nairobi, Kijabe Hills is one of Kenya's hidden hiking gems. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Great Rift Valley, indigenous forests, historic railway viewpoints, abundant birdlife, and fresh mountain air. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced hiker, Kijabe Hills provides an unforgettable day hike with experienced local guides from Kijabe Adventures.

Kijabe Hills is one of Kenya's hidden hiking gems, offering panoramic views of the Great Rift Valley, lush forests, historic railway viewpoints, and beginner-friendly trails. Guided hikes by Kijabe Adventures include local guides, safety support, group experiences, and private hikes.

  • Distance from Nairobi: About 1 hour
  • Difficulty: Easy–Moderate
  • Highlights: Rift Valley viewpoints, forests, wildlife, Old Kijabe township

Unlike many self-guided hiking trails near Nairobi, Kijabe Adventures provides experienced local guides, organized transport options, emergency support, photography stops, and a friendly hiking community. The trail is suitable for beginners, families, corporate teams, and experienced hikers looking to explore the Great Rift Valley.

2. Karura Forest

Karura Forest is one of the most popular hiking and walking destinations in Nairobi. With over 50 km of marked trails, it features waterfalls, bamboo groves, caves, and wildlife.

  • Distance from Nairobi CBD: 15 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy–Moderate
  • Highlights: Karura waterfall, caves, cycling trails

3. Ngong Hills

Ngong Hills offers one of the most scenic ridge hikes near Nairobi. The 7-hill trail is windy, refreshing, and full of panoramic views of the Rift Valley.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 35 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate
  • Highlights: Ridge views, wind turbines, Rift Valley scenery

4. Oloolua Nature Trail (Karen)

If you’re looking for a quiet retreat in nature, Oloolua is perfect. This forest sanctuary features caves, thick canopy cover, and a waterfall.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 30 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Waterfall, caves, picnic areas

what makes Oloolua Nature Hiking Trail best for children hiking adventures (Karen)

Oloolua Nature Trail is one of the safest hiking destinations near Nairobi for families with children. The well-maintained trails are relatively short, shaded by indigenous forest, and feature exciting attractions such as caves, a waterfall, suspension bridges, picnic areas, and abundant birdlife. The gentle terrain makes it an excellent introduction to hiking for young adventurers.

  • Short walking distances
  • Safe forest trails
  • Picnic sites
  • Educational nature experience
  • Excellent for school trips

5. Kiambu Forest

A peaceful, less-crowded forest ideal for long quiet walks, birdwatching, and photography.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 25 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Dense canopy, quiet trails, birdlife

6. Mount Longonot (Day Trip)

A more challenging but rewarding day hike offering crater views, volcanic landscapes, and Rift Valley scenery. Although outside Nairobi, it’s perfect for a weekend day trip.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 1 hr 15 min
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate–Hard
  • Highlights: Crater hike, panoramic Rift Valley views

7. Kereita Forest

Kereita Forest is a serene forest reserve located about 1.5 hours from Nairobi. It offers a variety of trails suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers. The forest is home to diverse wildlife, including monkeys and a variety of bird species, making it a great spot for nature enthusiasts.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 1 hr 30 min
  • Trail difficulty: Easy–Moderate
  • Highlights: Wildlife, birdwatching, peaceful forest trails

8. Kinale Forest

Kinale Forest is a unique hiking destination located about 2 hours from Nairobi. The trail takes hikers through rolling hills and open landscapes, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It’s a great option for those looking for a more remote and less crowded hiking experience.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 2 hours
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate
  • Highlights: Rolling hills, open landscapes, panoramic views

9. Aberdare National Park

Aberdare National Park is a stunning hiking destination located about 3 hours from Nairobi. The park features lush forests, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and various bird species. Hikers can explore well-marked trails that offer a mix of challenging terrain and breathtaking scenery.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 3 hours
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate–Hard
  • Highlights: Waterfalls, wildlife, lush forests

10. Olorgesailie Prehistoric Site

The Olorgesailie Prehistoric Site is a fascinating hiking destination located about 2.5 hours from Nairobi. The site is known for its archaeological significance, with ancient stone tools and fossils dating back hundreds of thousands of years. Hikers can explore the area while learning about the rich history and cultural heritage of the region.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 2.5 hours
  • Trail difficulty: Easy–Moderate
  • Highlights: Archaeological site, ancient tools, cultural heritage

How to Choose the Best Hike for You

  • For beginners: kijabe Hills, Karura, Oloolua, Kiambu Forest
  • For scenic views: Ngong Hills, Longonot
  • For a quiet escape: Kiambu Forest
  • For fitness training: Ngong Hills or Longonot

What to Pack for Nairobi Day Hikes

  • Comfortable hiking shoes
  • 2–3 liters of water
  • Snacks or energy bars
  • Sunscreen and hat
  • Light jacket
  • Camera or binoculars

Final Thoughts

Nairobi is one of the few cities in the world where you can enjoy spectacular hiking experiences just minutes away from urban life. Whether you prefer forests, hills, or waterfalls, there’s a perfect day hike waiting for you every weekend.

💡 Pro Tip: Visit Kijabe Hills early in the morning for the clearest views of the Great Rift Valley.
