Best Day Hikes Near Nairobi: Perfect for Weekends

Updated on March 2026 · 7–10 min read

Nairobi offers some of Kenya’s best day hikes—perfect for weekend explorers, fitness lovers, and nature enthusiasts. Whether you want waterfalls, panoramic hills, dense forests, or wildlife-filled trails, you don’t have to travel far.

Why Nairobi Is Perfect for Day Hikes

Nairobi’s strategic location places several nature reserves, mountains, and forests within 30–90 minutes of the city. Most trails are beginner-friendly, affordable, and accessible by public or private transport.

1. Karura Forest

Karura Forest is one of the most popular hiking and walking destinations in Nairobi. With over 50 km of marked trails, it features waterfalls, bamboo groves, caves, and wildlife.

  • Distance from Nairobi CBD: 15 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy–Moderate
  • Highlights: Karura waterfall, caves, cycling trails

2. Ngong Hills

Ngong Hills offers one of the most scenic ridge hikes near Nairobi. The 7-hill trail is windy, refreshing, and full of panoramic views of the Rift Valley.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 35 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate
  • Highlights: Ridge views, wind turbines, Rift Valley scenery

3. Oloolua Nature Trail (Karen)

If you’re looking for a quiet retreat in nature, Oloolua is perfect. This forest sanctuary features caves, thick canopy cover, and a waterfall.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 30 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Waterfall, caves, picnic areas

4. Kiambu Forest

A peaceful, less-crowded forest ideal for long quiet walks, birdwatching, and photography.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 25 minutes
  • Trail difficulty: Easy
  • Highlights: Dense canopy, quiet trails, birdlife

5. Mount Longonot (Day Trip)

A more challenging but rewarding day hike offering crater views, volcanic landscapes, and Rift Valley scenery. Although outside Nairobi, it’s perfect for a weekend day trip.

  • Distance from Nairobi: 1 hr 15 min
  • Trail difficulty: Moderate–Hard
  • Highlights: Crater hike, panoramic Rift Valley views

How to Choose the Best Hike for You

  • For beginners: Karura, Oloolua, Kiambu Forest
  • For scenic views: Ngong Hills, Longonot
  • For a quiet escape: Kiambu Forest
  • For fitness training: Ngong Hills or Longonot

What to Pack for Nairobi Day Hikes

  • Comfortable hiking shoes
  • 2–3 liters of water
  • Snacks or energy bars
  • Sunscreen and hat
  • Light jacket
  • Camera or binoculars

Final Thoughts

Nairobi is one of the few cities in the world where you can enjoy spectacular hiking experiences just minutes away from urban life. Whether you prefer forests, hills, or waterfalls, there’s a perfect day hike waiting for you every weekend.
