Partner, Invest & Grow with Kijabe Adventures

Kijabe Adventures combines outdoor tourism, environmental conservation, community empowerment, and sustainable energy solutions to create lasting impact across Kenya.

We welcome sponsors, donors, adventure tourism partners, conservation organizations, hiking brands, event organizers, and investors who want to help build world-class outdoor experiences while supporting local communities.

Why Partnership Matters

Kijabe Adventures is more than just a hiking group — we are a growing movement dedicated to:

  • ✔ Promoting *safe and responsible hiking* in Kenya
  • ✔ Supporting *environmental conservation* through tree planting & trail clean-ups
  • ✔ Training *local guides* and youth to create sustainable livelihoods
  • ✔ Restoring and maintaining hiking trails to protect local ecosystems
  • ✔ Building a community that values nature, wellness, and outdoor adventure

Every contribution — no matter the size — helps us keep hikers safe, protect natural areas, and empower communities.

♻ Sustainable Briquette Project

One of our flagship community projects is the production of eco-friendly charcoal briquettes made from sustainable biomass materials. The project helps reduce dependence on traditional charcoal, promotes forest conservation, creates employment opportunities, and provides cleaner fuel alternatives.

We are currently seeking support for:

  • Industrial briquette presses
  • Drying and carbonization equipment
  • Packaging and branding equipment
  • Production workspace improvements
  • Distribution and logistics support

Sponsors and investors may participate through equipment support, funding, technical expertise, or revenue-sharing agreements.

🥾 Join Our Guide Network

We are expanding our network of trained local hiking guides across Kenya. We're especially interested in guides near Ngong Hills, Kijabe Forest, Mount Longonot, Suswa, Aberdares, Karura Forest, Menengai Crater, and the Mount Kenya region.

If you are a professional guide or have extensive local trail knowledge, we would love to hear from you.
